Research Paper

TLDR: I conducted research at Northeastern University for the development of software for the Digilab Celljet. The Digilab Celljet is a 3D printer capable of printing live cells. I worked on developing a software package for the 3D printer so that we could easily convert 3D CAD models into discrete printable points for the Celljet.



Full Story: After conducting research at WPI over the summer, I was eager to find a research position when I got back to school. Having return to Northeastern University, I set out to find a research position. I managed to get connected to a professor who needed help with research, working on the Digilab Celljet. The Digilab Celljet is a 3D printer capable of printing live cells. I was tasked with the research and development of a new software package for the printer. After being shown around the lab, and a quick demo of the printer, I understood why improvements were necessary. The Celljet is controlled using G-code, and has built in software. However, this software does not allow for easy user input and generation of 3D models. I wanted to find a way to convert 3D CAD models into discrete printable points that the Celljet could use to print. I did extensive research on the different methods of 3D printing biological materials currently avaliable and read articles on each of their requirements. This research showed that there was no one uniform system for designing and printing biologics. Our research team hoped to create a new standard in live tissue printing.
